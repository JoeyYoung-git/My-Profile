Project Idea

Overview

5 pour coffee maker inspired byTestu Kasuya (World Brewers Cup 2016 Champion)

The aim of my project will to be create a coffee machine that replicates the method of pour over coffee that Testu used to win him the World Brewers Cup championship.

This is a multi-stage, very specific way of pouring coffee that enhances certain flavours and scents from the coffee

Motivation

The motivation for this project was that each morning it takes about 10-15 minutes to make my coffee.

Between manually grinding fresh beans to letting the hot water sit for a timed period. It would be very convenient to be able to have a machine that makes the same great coffee.

Something to keep in mind during the process is to maintain the quality as best I can while automating the processes

Description

This machine will be a coffee machine inspired by Testu Kasuya as you can see in this video

A similar project I can refer to for some code will be github teasmade

Method:
  1. The process begins with using a container for the coffee beans that has the capability to suction air out of the container to keep our beans fresh. Something like a customised version of the Fellow Atmos Vacuum Caniser
  2. For extra convenience I will connect raspberry pi to google calendar in order to begin the coffee machine as the morning alarm goes off. We can set a trigger word as “Morning Alarm”.
  3. The next step is to begin the machine boiling 300ml of water. (We can create an option of getting the machine plumbed into a water supply (Tee off from the water supply under the kitchen sink and connect with beverage tube) or having a manually filled cannister. I will connect a mini heating element to our power supply and screw mount it into the water cannister. This will need to also be temperature controlled by a thermometer and have an on/off switch
  4. It will then drop out a measured 20g of coffee beans into the grinder section of the machine. (I’m going to use a raspberry pi candy dispenserfor this) I will need to time the stepper motor for the opening so that exactly 20g drops from the container as accurate as possible.
  5. The beans will be coarsely ground by an automatic grinder into a stainless-steel filter cone. Above where the coffee will be dispensed.
  6. The machine will begin the first 60ml pour, in a spiral motion to cover the surface of the beans. And then let it sit for 45 seconds.
  7. Repeat this process for 5x 45 second pours
  8. Your cup of coffee is ready. Bluetooth to wireless speaker and play this song
